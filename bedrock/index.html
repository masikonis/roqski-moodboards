The firm represents businesses, shareholders, and partners in commercial disputes across Texas. That includes breach of contract, fiduciary duty claims, fraud, partnership and shareholder disputes, and injunctive relief like temporary restraining orders.

Court presence

Attorneys carry active caseloads in Dallas, Tarrant, Collin, and Denton county courts, plus the Northern and Eastern Districts of Texas. They know the local rules and scheduling patterns in each courthouse, which means fewer procedural surprises.

Every matter gets a dedicated team: a lead attorney, a supporting attorney, and a paralegal. Clients get portal access for real-time case updates and document sharing. The attorneys who build the strategy are the same ones who carry it out.

What the team structure looks like

The lead attorney sets strategy, manages court appearances, and is the primary point of contact. The supporting attorney handles research, motion drafting, and discovery. The paralegal coordinates filings, deadlines, and client communication.

The firm also advises on business transactions, entity formation, corporate governance, and due diligence. The idea is to structure things correctly up front, and to handle the disputes that come up when prior structuring didn't.

Asset purchases and sales, entity formation (LLC, corporation, partnership), operating agreements and shareholder agreements, commercial lease review, due diligence for acquisitions, and contract drafting and negotiation.
