Business owners don't call a lawyer because things are going well. Something broke — a contract, a partnership, a deal — and now the business is exposed.

The firm's job starts with understanding what broke and why it matters to the business. Not just the legal question, but the operational one. What does this cost you every week it's unresolved? What does a win actually look like?

That kind of understanding only comes from attorneys who ask the right questions early and stay close to the case throughout.

Business litigation means working within the procedural and substantive rules that govern commercial disputes: partnership law, fiduciary obligations, contract interpretation under the Texas Business & Commerce Code. A general practitioner handles these occasionally. A business litigation firm handles them every day.

The firm has attorneys in Dallas, Fort Worth, McKinney, Mansfield, Houston, Austin, and San Antonio. They know the local rules, the scheduling patterns, and the procedural expectations in each courthouse. That means fewer surprises regardless of where a case is filed.

Each matter gets a team: a lead attorney, a supporting attorney, and a paralegal. Clients get direct communication channels and access to the firm's case portal. The people who build the strategy are the same people who carry it out.

The firm's practice spans business litigation, real estate disputes, intellectual property matters, and business transactions — from initial structuring through trial, if it comes to that.
